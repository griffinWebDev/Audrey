My Story

Hi, my name is Audrey Harty. Thank you for taking an interest in my website, and I hope you’re enjoying it. Some fun facts about me are that my favorite season is winter, my favorite activity is skiing, I’m a Gemini, I am studying French and I don’t like tomatoes.

I was raised in the mountains of Park City Utah, but my parents are originally from Minnesota and Iowa. They moved here for the skiing and put me on a pair of skis shortly after I could stand up. We would ski every weekend and I always wanted one of the warm fresh chocolate chip cookies when we went in the ski lodge for lunch.

My family values food as an experience, rather than just a necessity and that’s how I started to develop my sense of taste. Always observing flavors and textures as I ate, instead of just swallowing so I could have the next bite.

My Dad became a pastry chef at Deer Valley resort and when I was 14, I got a job there too. My job started as chopping up fruit and placing it in bowls for the dessert fondue at one of the restaurants, not very exciting but it was a foot in the door.

I’m an artist at heart and my dream was to do the wedding cakes made at the resort in the summer. Five years past and at 19 I went to culinary school at Cascade culinary institute in Bend, Oregon for the baking and pastry degree. I was there for a whole year each class lasting three months, with several classes each quarter.

My favorite classes were artisan bread, cake decorating, and culinary math. I quickly realized school wasn’t for me and instead of staying 6 more months for the associates, I left with the one year certificate. I returned home, missing the comfort of the mountains, and continued my education on the job.

That summer I finally got to be part of the wedding cake team and loved it. The following seasons I acquired more responsibility and trained new bakers until finally in 2018 I decided to jump ship and leave the Deer valley bakery in search of new experiences.

I got a job as the pastry chef at the Goldener Hirsch Inn, a high end lunch and dinner restaurant attached to the hotel with the same name. It was my first time really running the show and designing plated desserts. I knew I didn’t know what I was getting myself into and it was a maybe over ambitious move, but I felt the need to push myself way out of my comfort zone that I had been in for so long. Personally and professionally I grew immensely that winter.

My dessert menu included their classic Apple Cranberry Strudel with my own spin on it, a Chocolate Mousse Cake with a French Mousse, a Pear Frangipane Tart with pears poached in a sweet gewurztraminer wine, chocolate fondue platter served with fruit, poundcake and house made marshmallows, a cookie platter with chocolate chip cookies, double chocolate chip cookies and toffee pecan cookies, and a quiver of house made ice creams and sorbets, my favorite being the popcorn and browned butter ice cream. We also made home-made pretzels and brioche pull apart bread daily.

Next I'm off to the big island of Hawaii to travel and work for the summer.

My dream is to one day work in a patisserie in France by the Pyrenees Mountains or the Alps.
